Alternative Vote

Alternative Vote is a system of electing candidates used in countries such as Australia.

The voters are presented with a ballot where they rank candidates in order of preference. When the ballots have been collected, the votes will be distributed to the first-preference candidate of the voters. Then, if there is no candidate with a majority, the votes for the lowest candidate get distributed to the voters' second-choice candidate. This process of eliminating the candidate with the least votes and redistributing the votes to the voters' next choice candidates continues until one candidate has a majority of votes.


Pros:

There are some pros to this system

  • The outcome is at least acceptable to most people
  • It eliminates the need for tactical voting so it allows for many parties to win seats.

Cons:

There are, however, many cons to this system:

  • Generally, only moderate candidates will win elections
  • More than likely, most voters will not get all their views properly represented.
